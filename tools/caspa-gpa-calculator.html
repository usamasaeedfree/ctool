Why is Calculating Your CASPA GPA Important?

PA school admissions are highly competitive, with most programs requiring a minimum GPA between 3.0 and 3.5. Your CASPA GPA calculation often differs from your institution's GPA because:

  • CASPA includes all course attempts (including retakes)
  • They use a standardized grading scale across all institutions
  • They categorize courses differently than your college might

Using our accurate CASPA GPA calculator ensures you understand your actual standing before applying.

Tips for Improving Your CASPA Science GPA

If your CASPA GPA is lower than expected, consider these strategies:

  • Retake prerequisite courses with grades below B-
  • Take additional upper-level science courses to demonstrate capability
  • Focus on strengthening other areas of your application (patient care experience, personal statement, letters of recommendation)
  • Apply to programs where your GPA is within their accepted range

CASPA Science GPA Calculation FAQs

What is the difference between Science GPA and BCP GPA?

Science GPA = All natural science courses.
BCP GPA = Biology, Chemistry, Physics only (subset of Science GPA).

Does CASPA include +/- grades?

Yes. CASPA factors in plus/minus grades (A-, B+, etc.).

How does CASPA handle repeated courses?

CASPA includes all attempts — they do not replace old grades.

What counts as a science course?

Biology, chemistry, physics, anatomy, physiology, and other natural sciences.

Does CASPA round GPA?

No. CASPA reports GPA to two decimal places.

What is a competitive CASPA GPA?

Most PA programs prefer 3.4–3.6+ overall GPA with a 3.5+ science GPA.

How do I calculate GPA if my course is pass/fail?

CASPA includes “Pass” credits but they do not affect GPA.

Is the Last 45 Credits GPA important?

Yes, especially if your earlier GPA is lower. Schools use it to evaluate academic improvement.

What happens if my university GPA is different from CASPA GPA?

CASPA GPA is the one that admissions committees use. Your school GPA doesn’t matter in the CASPA application.

Do all PA programs have the same GPA requirement?

No. Some require 3.0 minimum, others are more competitive (3.5+ average accepted GPA).

What is the difference between Science GPA and BCP GPA?

Science GPA includes all science courses (biology, chemistry, physics, etc.), while BCP GPA specifically includes only biology, chemistry, and physics courses. BCP is a subset of Science GPA.

Does CASPA include +/- grades in their calculation?

Yes, CASPA includes plus/minus grades in their GPA calculation, which is why our calculator offers these precise options.

How does CASPA handle repeated courses?

CASPA includes all attempts of repeated courses in your GPA calculation, unlike some institutional GPA calculations that might replace the grade.

What counts as a science course for CASPA?

CASPA considers biology, chemistry, physics, and other natural sciences (including health sciences like anatomy and physiology) as science courses.

How does CASPA calculate GPA?

CASPA calculates GPA using all course attempts, standardized grading, and a plus/minus scale. Our tool follows this method exactly.
