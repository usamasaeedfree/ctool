Understanding CGPA and GPA

GPA stands for Grade Point Average. It is the average grade point average of the subjects taken in a semester. In simple terms, GPA gives you a snapshot of your performance in a semester.

CGPA stands for Cumulative Grade Point Average. It is the average grade point average of all the semesters of your entire course/degree. It gives an idea of your overall academic performance.

Example:

If your first semester GPA is 3.5 and your second semester GPA is 3.8, then your CGPA will be the combined average of these two semesters.

GPA indicates short-term performance, while CGPA is a measure of long-term performance.

It is important to understand both of them because CGPA/GPA matters a lot for universities, scholarships, and jobs. Use our UAF CGPA Calculator App for instant results.
