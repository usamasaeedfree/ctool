The UCP Calculator is a free online tool specifically designed for students of the University of Central Punjab to help them track and evaluate their academic performance.

What is UCP Grade Percentage?

The UCP Grade Percentage refers to the percentage of marks a student has obtained out of the total marks in a subject or semester at the University of Central Punjab (UCP). This percentage is then converted into a grade (A, B+, etc.) and a GPA value according to the official UCP grading policy.


What is UCP Percentage Criteria?

UCP’s percentage criteria is the system that defines which percentage range corresponds to which letter grade and GPA.

For example:
  • 85% and above = A grade
  • 80% - 84% = A- grade
  • 75% - 79% = B+ grade

This ensures grading is standardized for all students and departments.


How is CGPA Calculated in UCP?

At UCP, CGPA (Cumulative Grade Point Average) is calculated by:

  1. Multiplying the grade point of each subject by its credit hours (this gives quality points).
  2. Adding all quality points together.
  3. Dividing the total quality points by the total credit hours attempted.

Example:
If you have 3 subjects with grades worth 3.5, 3.0, and 3.7 GPA points and each has 3 credit hours, the CGPA is calculated accordingly.


What is UCP Grading Policy?

The UCP grading policy defines the percentage ranges, letter grades, and GPA points that determine student performance. It also outlines rules for probation, honors, and degree completion. This policy ensures fairness and transparency in the evaluation process.


UCP Grading Policy 2025

In 2025, UCP updated its grading policy to better align with international academic standards. The changes mainly included:

  • Adjusted percentage ranges for certain grades.
  • Clearer rules for GPA rounding.
  • Updated probation and dismissal criteria.

You can check the official policy on UCP’s website for complete details.
