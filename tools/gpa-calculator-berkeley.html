Berkeley GPA Scale (Official UC Berkeley Grading System)

Here’s the official GPA scale used by UC Berkeley to convert letter grades to grade points.

Grade Letter Grade Point Percentage Range
A+ 4.0 97–100%
A 4.0 93–96%

UC Berkeley Non-Letter Grades and Notations

UC Berkeley also uses several non-letter grades to represent a student’s progress or course status. These grades don’t affect your GPA directly but provide important academic information. Here’s what each notation means:

Grade / Notation Meaning GPA Effect Applies To
P (Pass) Represents performance of C- or better. Not counted toward GPA calculation. Undergraduate
NP (Not Pass) Represents performance below C- level. Not counted toward GPA calculation. Undergraduate
S (Satisfactory) Indicates B- or better work. Not counted toward GPA calculation. Graduate
U (Unsatisfactory) Represents work below B- level. Not counted toward GPA calculation. Graduate
I (Incomplete) Given when coursework is incomplete due to uncontrollable circumstances but otherwise passing. Not included in GPA (after Fall 1973). All Students
IP (In Progress) Assigned when a multi-term course is still in progress. Final grade awarded after completion. Not counted toward GPA. All Students
NR (Not Reported) Temporary administrative mark when no grade is submitted. Not counted toward GPA. All Students
M (Missing) Temporary administrative symbol until grade is finalized. Not counted toward GPA. All Students
RD (Review Deferred) Used when a grade is temporarily withheld pending review. Not counted toward GPA. All Students

Note: These designations appear on official UC Berkeley transcripts but are excluded from grade-point computations. Always confirm current policies on the UC Berkeley Registrar’s website.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the UC Berkeley GPA Calculator?

The UC Berkeley GPA Calculator is an online tool that helps students and applicants estimate their semester or cumulative GPA using Berkeley’s official 4.0 grading scale.

How does the UC Berkeley GPA scale work?

UC Berkeley uses a 4.0 grading scale where an A or A+ equals 4.0 points, B equals 3.0, C equals 2.0, D equals 1.0, and F equals 0.0. Plus and minus grades (like A– = 3.7) slightly adjust the value.

What GPA do I need to get into UC Berkeley?

Most admitted freshmen have GPAs between 3.8 and 4.0 (unweighted). California residents must have at least a 3.0 GPA to apply, while non-residents need a minimum of 3.4.

Does UC Berkeley count Pass/No Pass grades in GPA?

No. Pass (P) and Not Pass (NP) grades don’t affect your UC Berkeley GPA. They simply show that you passed or did not pass a course.

Can graduate students use this GPA Calculator?

Yes. Graduate students can use this calculator to estimate their academic standing based on the same 4.0 scale used in most Berkeley graduate programs.

Is this calculator officially provided by UC Berkeley?

No. This is an unofficial tool created for educational purposes using UC Berkeley’s official grading standards. It’s designed to help students understand and track their performance.
